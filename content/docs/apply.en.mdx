---
title: Apply for admission
description: Subtitle groups apply by email; identity is verified manually before publishing access is granted.
---

AniBT does not self-serve publishing accounts. **Subtitle groups** apply by email and are reviewed manually. Once admitted we open a publishing account on your existing AniBT user, hook up the group's identity, and from there you can publish via the [Release API](/docs/open-api/releases-publish) and [sync to other sites](/docs/site-sync).

## What to send

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Email from your registered address
    Send the application from the email address you used to register on AniBT. We use it to confirm you are the account holder and not someone applying in your name.

    Haven't registered yet? Sign up at [anibt.net](https://anibt.net) first, then write in from that same address.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Group basics
    Include in the email body:

    - **Group name**: full subtitle group name.
    - **slug**: a short, stable English identifier used as the unique ID, e.g. `sakura-sub`. Pick something you won't want to change — it shows up in public URLs like [`/rss/group/<slug>.xml`](/docs/open-api/other-endpoints).
    - **Short intro**: a paragraph or two about what you release, how long you've been active, and your current pace.
    - **Contact channels**: Telegram, group website, Discord, mailing list — anything we can reach the group through.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Identity proof
    Provide **one** of the following — you don't need all three:

    - The publishing account / email your group already uses on nyaa, ACG.rip, Mikan, etc., so we can cross-check.
    - Admin proof on the group's official site / Telegram channel / Twitter (e.g. a temporary pinned post acknowledging the application).
    - Other evidence widely recognised among existing fansub members.

    The point is to confirm you actually represent the group and aren't squatting someone else's slug.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Send to

<Callout type="info">
  Email **[support@anibt.net](mailto:support@anibt.net)**. A subject line like `Group application - <name>` helps it land in the right queue. Replies usually go out within a few working days.
</Callout>

If you don't hear anything within a week (check spam), feel free to ping us again or reach out via [other channels](/docs/contact).

## After approval

We reply by email confirming the account is set up. On your side:

- Your group identity and slug are now bound to your account.
- The [Release API](/docs/open-api/releases-publish) becomes available — you'll receive an API Key.
- Your group becomes visible in the site listings and the [group RSS feeds](/docs/open-api/other-endpoints).
